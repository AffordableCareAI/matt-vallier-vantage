Understanding ACA Coverage

How ACA Health Insurance Works

The Affordable Care Act (ACA) created the Health Insurance Marketplace where individuals, families, and small businesses can shop for health coverage. Every ACA plan is required to cover 10 essential health benefits, and no one can be denied coverage or charged more because of a pre-existing condition.

There are two main windows to enroll in marketplace coverage:

  • Open Enrollment Period (OEP) — The annual window, typically November 1 through January 15, when anyone can enroll in or change their marketplace health plan. Plans selected by December 15 start January 1.
  • Special Enrollment Period (SEP) — A 60-day window triggered by qualifying life events: losing other coverage, marriage, birth or adoption of a child, moving to a new coverage area, or losing Medicaid eligibility.
  • Premium Tax Credits — Financial assistance that lowers your monthly premium based on household income. Available to individuals and families earning between 100% and 400% of the Federal Poverty Level.
  • Cost-Sharing Reductions (CSR) — Extra savings on Silver plans that lower your deductibles, copays, and out-of-pocket maximum. Available if your household income is below 250% of FPL.

Many people in South Florida qualify for significant premium assistance and do not realize it. A family of four earning $60,000 per year may qualify for hundreds of dollars per month in premium tax credits. Matt Vallier calculates your exact subsidy amount and ensures you are getting every dollar of assistance you are entitled to.

Plan Comparison

ACA Metal Tier Plans

All ACA plans cover the same essential benefits. The metal tier determines how costs are split between you and the insurer.

Bronze

60/40

Plan pays 60% — you pay 40%

  • Lowest monthly premium — best for people who rarely use healthcare
  • Highest deductible — you pay more out of pocket before insurance kicks in
  • Good for healthy individuals who want catastrophic protection at the lowest cost

Silver

70/30

Plan pays 70% — you pay 30%

  • Moderate premium, moderate deductible — balanced cost-sharing
  • CSR eligible — the only tier that qualifies for Cost-Sharing Reductions, lowering your deductible and copays
  • Best value for most people — especially if you qualify for CSR subsidies

Gold

80/20

Plan pays 80% — you pay 20%

  • Higher monthly premium — but significantly lower deductible
  • Lower out-of-pocket costs — you pay less when you actually use healthcare services
  • Good for regular healthcare users — families, people with ongoing prescriptions or specialist visits

Platinum

90/10

Plan pays 90% — you pay 10%

  • Highest monthly premium — but the lowest deductible and out-of-pocket maximum
  • Lowest cost when you need care — minimal copays and coinsurance
  • Best for heavy healthcare users — multiple prescriptions, chronic conditions, frequent specialist visits

Not sure which metal tier fits your situation? Matt Vallier will analyze your healthcare usage, prescription needs, and budget to recommend the plan that minimizes your total annual cost — not just your premium.

Health Insurance FAQ

Straight answers to the questions we hear most about ACA and health insurance.

The annual Open Enrollment Period for ACA marketplace plans typically runs from November 1 through January 15. If you select a plan by December 15, your coverage starts January 1. Plans selected between December 16 and January 15 take effect February 1. Outside of open enrollment, you can only enroll if you qualify for a Special Enrollment Period triggered by a qualifying life event — losing other coverage, getting married, having a baby, moving to a new area, or losing Medicaid eligibility. Matt Vallier can help you determine if you qualify for a special enrollment period and get you enrolled quickly.
Premium tax credits are available to individuals and families with household income between 100% and 400% of the Federal Poverty Level who purchase coverage through the ACA marketplace. For 2026, a single person earning up to approximately $58,320 may qualify. For a family of four, the threshold is around $120,000. The subsidy amount is based on a sliding scale — the lower your income, the larger your subsidy. If your income falls below 250% of FPL, you may also qualify for Cost-Sharing Reductions on Silver plans that lower your deductibles and copays. Matt Vallier can calculate your exact subsidy amount based on your projected income and help you choose the plan that maximizes your savings.
The metal tiers represent how costs are shared between you and the insurance company. Bronze plans have the lowest premiums but cover only about 60% of costs — you pay 40% through deductibles, copays, and coinsurance. Silver plans cover 70% and are the only tier eligible for Cost-Sharing Reductions that lower your deductible and copays. Gold plans cover 80% with higher premiums but lower out-of-pocket costs when you use care. Platinum plans cover 90% with the highest premiums but the lowest deductibles and copays. All metal tiers cover the same 10 essential health benefits including hospitalization, prescription drugs, maternity care, mental health, and preventive services. The right choice depends on how often you use healthcare and whether you prefer lower premiums or lower costs at the doctor.
