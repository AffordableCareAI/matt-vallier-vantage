What Is a D-SNP?

A Dual Eligible Special Needs Plan (D-SNP) is a type of Medicare Advantage plan built specifically for people who qualify for both Medicare and Medicaid.

D-SNP plans combine your Medicare and Medicaid benefits into a single, coordinated plan. Instead of navigating two separate programs with different rules, providers, and paperwork, a D-SNP gives you one card, one plan, and a dedicated care coordinator who understands both programs.

These plans are offered by private insurance carriers approved by Medicare. In Southeast Florida, D-SNP plans are available from Humana, UnitedHealthcare, Aetna, Devoted Health, and WellCare. Each carrier offers different benefit levels, so comparing plans is important.

D-SNP plans often include benefits that go far beyond what Original Medicare provides — dental work, eyeglasses, hearing aids, rides to the doctor, a monthly allowance for over-the-counter health products, and even meal delivery after hospital stays.

Who Is Dual Eligible?

You are dual eligible if you have Medicare Part A and Part B AND you are enrolled in Medicaid. This includes people with full Medicaid benefits and those with partial Medicaid benefits like the Medicare Savings Program (QMB, SLMB, QI). If you receive both a Medicare card and a Medicaid card, you are likely dual eligible and may qualify for a D-SNP.

D-SNP Benefits Beyond Original Medicare

D-SNP plans are loaded with extra benefits designed for dual eligible individuals. Here are the most common benefits included.

🗡

$0 or Low Premiums

Most D-SNP plans in Florida have a $0 monthly premium. Your Medicare and Medicaid benefits work together to minimize out-of-pocket costs.

🦷

Dental Coverage

Comprehensive dental including cleanings, fillings, extractions, dentures, and sometimes implants. Annual maximums often reach $2,000 or more.

👓

Vision Coverage

Annual eye exams, prescription eyeglasses or contact lenses, and frames allowance. Some plans cover lens upgrades at no extra cost.

🔊

Hearing Benefits

Hearing exams, hearing aid fittings, and a hearing aid allowance of $500 to $3,000 per year depending on the carrier and plan.

🚕

Transportation

Rides to and from medical appointments, pharmacies, and other healthcare-related locations. Many plans offer 24 or more one-way trips per year.

💊

OTC Allowance

A monthly or quarterly allowance for over-the-counter health products like vitamins, pain relievers, first aid supplies, and personal care items.

Eligibility & Enrollment

Qualifying for a D-SNP requires both Medicare and Medicaid. Here is what you need and how enrollment works.

Requirements

  • Must have Medicare Part A and Part B
  • Must be enrolled in your state's Medicaid program (full or partial)
  • Must live in the plan's service area
  • Includes QMB, SLMB, QI, and full dual eligible beneficiaries

Enrollment Flexibility

Dual eligible individuals have more enrollment flexibility than regular Medicare beneficiaries. You can enroll in, switch, or drop a D-SNP plan once per quarter during the first three quarters of the year (January through September). You can also enroll during the Annual Enrollment Period (October 15 through December 7).

Coordinated Care Between Medicare & Medicaid

One of the biggest advantages of a D-SNP is having a single point of contact who coordinates your care across both programs.

Managing two separate insurance programs is confusing. Medicare covers some services, Medicaid covers others, and figuring out which program pays for what can be overwhelming. A D-SNP eliminates that confusion by coordinating everything through one plan.

Your D-SNP plan assigns you a care coordinator who understands both Medicare and Medicaid rules. They help you find providers, schedule appointments, manage medications, and navigate benefits. This coordination leads to better health outcomes and fewer gaps in care.

D-SNP plans also coordinate your prescription drug coverage through Medicare Part D, which is built into the plan. Your care coordinator ensures that your medications are covered and helps identify lower-cost alternatives when they are available.

For people managing multiple chronic conditions, a D-SNP's integrated approach to care means fewer missed appointments, better medication adherence, and a single team that understands your complete health picture.
