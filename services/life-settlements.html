What Is a Life Settlement?

A life settlement is a legal, regulated transaction where you sell your existing life insurance policy to a licensed institutional buyer for a lump-sum cash payment.

When you sell your policy through a life settlement, you receive a cash payment that is more than the policy's cash surrender value but less than the full death benefit. The buyer takes over the policy, pays future premiums, and becomes the beneficiary.

Life settlements are regulated by the state of Florida and are a fully legal financial transaction. The industry has existed for over two decades and is backed by institutional investors including pension funds, endowments, and licensed life settlement providers.

Many policyholders do not realize their policy has significant market value. Policies that are no longer needed, no longer affordable, or part of a changed estate plan may qualify for a life settlement that puts real money back in your pocket.

Common Reasons People Sell

Premiums have become too expensive. The policy is no longer needed because a spouse has passed away or children are financially independent. Retirement income is needed now more than a future death benefit. Estate planning goals have changed. A term policy is about to expire and will lose all value. Health changes have made other financial priorities more urgent.

Who Qualifies for a Life Settlement?

Life settlements are not for everyone. Here are the general qualifications that make a policy eligible for sale on the secondary market.

Typical Qualifications

  • Policyholder is typically 65 years of age or older
  • Policy face value is $100,000 or more
  • Policy type: term (convertible), whole life, universal life, or variable life
  • A change in health status since the policy was issued
  • A change in financial needs or estate planning goals
  • Policy premiums have become difficult to afford

Factors That Increase Value

The value of a life settlement depends on several factors. Older policyholders with health changes typically receive higher offers because the expected payout timeline is shorter for buyers. Larger face value policies are more attractive to institutional buyers. Policies with lower ongoing premiums relative to the death benefit generate higher returns for buyers, which translates to higher offers for you.

Life Settlement vs. Surrendering Your Policy

If you no longer need your life insurance, you have two options: surrender the policy to the insurance company or sell it through a life settlement. The difference in payout can be dramatic.

Surrendering to Insurer

$15,000

Cash surrender value — this is what the insurance company pays you when you cancel your policy. It is a fraction of the policy's true market value.

The insurance company keeps the difference between what they pay you and what they would have paid in death benefits. They are not required to tell you about the life settlement option.

Life Settlement

$60,000 – $120,000

Life settlement value — this is what institutional buyers will pay on the secondary market. Typically 4 to 8 times the surrender value.

Multiple buyers compete for your policy, driving the price up. You receive the best offer, and the buyer takes over all future premium payments. You walk away with significantly more cash.
