What Is a C-SNP?

A Chronic Condition Special Needs Plan (C-SNP) is a type of Medicare Advantage plan designed for people who live with specific severe or disabling chronic conditions.

Unlike a standard Medicare Advantage plan that serves a general population, a C-SNP focuses its entire benefits structure around the needs of people with chronic diseases. The provider networks include specialists experienced with your condition. The drug formularies prioritize medications you are most likely to need. And the care coordination teams understand the day-to-day realities of managing a chronic illness.

C-SNP plans are offered by private insurance carriers approved by Medicare. In Southeast Florida, Humana, UnitedHealthcare, and Aetna offer C-SNP plans for various qualifying conditions. Each plan is tailored to a specific condition or group of conditions, so the benefits are directly relevant to your health situation.

To enroll in a C-SNP, you must have a qualifying chronic condition verified by your physician. Matt Vallier can walk you through the qualification process and help you compare available plans to find the one that best fits your specific condition and current medications.

How C-SNPs Differ from Regular Medicare Advantage

Regular Medicare Advantage plans serve everyone. C-SNPs are restricted to people with specific chronic conditions, which means the plan can focus all of its resources on the health needs most relevant to you. This results in better care coordination, more relevant provider networks, lower medication costs, and disease management programs designed for your exact condition.

Qualifying Chronic Conditions

Medicare approves C-SNP plans for specific chronic conditions. Here are the most common qualifying conditions available in Southeast Florida.

Diabetes Mellitus

Type 1 and Type 2 diabetes. C-SNP plans cover insulin, glucose monitors, test strips, diabetic shoes, and retinal screenings with low or $0 copays. Includes nutritional counseling and diabetes management programs.

Chronic Heart Failure

Congestive heart failure and related cardiac conditions. Plans include cardiac specialists, remote monitoring programs, medication management for heart failure drugs, and cardiac rehabilitation services.

COPD & Chronic Lung Disorders

Chronic obstructive pulmonary disease, emphysema, and chronic bronchitis. Coverage includes pulmonary specialists, respiratory therapy, inhalers and nebulizer medications, and pulmonary rehabilitation.

Cardiovascular Disorders

Coronary artery disease, atrial fibrillation, and other cardiovascular conditions. Plans provide cardiologist access, anticoagulation management, cardiac monitoring, and preventive care focused on heart health.

Chronic Kidney Disease

End-stage renal disease and chronic kidney disease requiring ongoing management. Coverage includes nephrology specialists, dialysis services, kidney transplant coordination, and dietary counseling.

Other Qualifying Conditions

Additional conditions may include dementia, HIV/AIDS, severe mental illness, and autoimmune disorders. Availability varies by carrier and plan year. Contact Matt Vallier to check if your specific condition qualifies.

How to Enroll in a C-SNP

Enrolling in a C-SNP requires a qualifying chronic condition verified by your doctor. Here is how the process works.

Enrollment Steps

  • Confirm you have Medicare Part A and Part B
  • Identify a C-SNP plan available for your qualifying condition in your area
  • Your physician completes a verification form confirming your diagnosis
  • Submit your enrollment application with the physician verification
  • Coverage begins as soon as the first day of the following month

Enrollment Flexibility

If you have a qualifying chronic condition, you may be eligible for a Special Enrollment Period that allows you to enroll outside of the Annual Enrollment Period. This means you do not have to wait until October to make a change.

You can also enroll during the Annual Enrollment Period (October 15 through December 7) or the Medicare Advantage Open Enrollment Period (January 1 through March 31). Matt Vallier handles the paperwork and coordinates with your physician to make the process as simple as possible.
