Why Supplemental Coverage Matters

Original Medicare (Parts A and B) covers hospital stays and doctor visits, but it provides little to no coverage for routine dental work, eye exams, glasses, hearing tests, or hearing aids. That gap can cost you thousands of dollars each year.

Nearly 65% of Medicare beneficiaries have no dental coverage at all. Without insurance, a single crown can cost $800 to $1,500. A pair of hearing aids runs $1,000 to $6,000. Even a basic pair of prescription glasses averages $200 to $400 out of pocket.

These are not luxury expenses. Untreated dental problems lead to infections and hospitalizations. Uncorrected vision impairs daily living and increases fall risk. Untreated hearing loss is linked to cognitive decline and social isolation.

The good news: supplemental plans from carriers like Humana, Aetna, UnitedHealthcare, and Cigna can cover preventive care, basic procedures, and even major services at affordable monthly premiums.

Who Needs Supplemental Coverage?

Anyone on Original Medicare who wants dental, vision, or hearing benefits. Anyone on a Medicare Advantage plan who wants additional coverage beyond what their plan provides. Anyone turning 65 who wants to maintain the dental and vision benefits they had through employer coverage.

Three Types of Supplemental Coverage

Each type addresses a different gap in Medicare. You can purchase them individually or bundled together for the best value.

Dental Insurance

Protect your oral health

What Is Covered

Dental insurance for Medicare beneficiaries covers the preventive, basic, and major dental services that Original Medicare does not. Most plans are structured in tiers, with preventive care covered at the highest percentage and major work at a lower percentage.

  • Preventive cleanings, exams, and X-rays (typically 100% covered)
  • Basic procedures: fillings, extractions, root canals (50-80% covered)
  • Major procedures: crowns, bridges, dentures (50% covered)
  • Some plans cover implants and orthodontics
  • Annual maximums typically $1,000 to $2,500

Who Needs It

Anyone on Original Medicare who wants routine dental care covered. Seniors who need dentures, crowns, or bridgework. People with diabetes, heart disease, or other conditions where oral health directly impacts overall health.

Vision Insurance

Keep your world in focus

What Is Covered

Vision insurance covers the routine eye care services and corrective lenses that Medicare does not. Plans typically cover annual exams, prescription eyeglasses, contact lenses, and discounts on procedures like LASIK.

  • Annual comprehensive eye exams (typically $0-$15 copay)
  • Prescription eyeglasses: frames and lenses allowance
  • Contact lens allowance (in lieu of glasses)
  • Discounts on lens upgrades: progressives, anti-glare, transitions
  • Retinal screening for diabetic eye disease

Who Needs It

Anyone who wears glasses or contact lenses. Seniors with diabetes or other conditions that require regular eye monitoring. People who want an annual eye exam covered without paying $200 or more out of pocket.

Hearing Insurance

Stay connected to the world

What Is Covered

Hearing insurance covers the exams, fittings, and hearing aids that Original Medicare excludes. With one in three Americans over 65 experiencing hearing loss, this coverage addresses one of the most expensive gaps in Medicare.

  • Annual hearing exams and screenings
  • Hearing aid allowance ($500-$3,000 per year)
  • Hearing aid fittings and adjustments
  • Battery and accessory coverage
  • Follow-up visits and reprogramming

Who Needs It

Anyone experiencing hearing loss or difficulty following conversations. Seniors who have been told they need hearing aids but are put off by the cost. People who want annual hearing screenings to catch changes early before they lead to cognitive decline or social withdrawal.

How Bundled Plans Work

Instead of purchasing three separate policies, a bundled plan combines dental, vision, and hearing coverage under a single premium. Monthly costs typically range from $25 to $60 depending on the carrier and benefit levels you choose.

Bundled plans simplify your paperwork with one monthly payment, one insurance card, and one customer service number. They are available year-round with no enrollment period restrictions for standalone supplemental plans.

Medicare Advantage vs. Standalone Supplemental Plans

Many Medicare Advantage plans include some dental, vision, and hearing benefits. But the coverage level varies dramatically from plan to plan. Here is what you need to know.

Medicare Advantage Benefits

Most Medicare Advantage plans in Florida include basic dental and vision as built-in benefits. However, these benefits are often limited to preventive care only: one or two cleanings per year, a basic eye exam, and a small frame allowance.

Major dental work like crowns, bridges, and dentures may have significant copays or annual limits as low as $1,000. Hearing aid allowances on Medicare Advantage plans range widely from $0 to $2,500 depending on the carrier and plan tier.

If your Medicare Advantage plan's dental, vision, or hearing benefits are not sufficient, you can add standalone supplemental coverage on top of your existing plan.

Standalone Supplemental Plans

Standalone plans offer more robust coverage and higher annual maximums than what most Medicare Advantage plans provide. You can purchase them at any time of year, and they work alongside any Medicare plan type.

Standalone dental plans often provide annual maximums of $1,500 to $2,500 with coverage for implants. Vision plans typically include larger frame and lens allowances. Hearing plans may offer $2,000 to $3,000 in hearing aid benefits per year.

An independent agent like Matt Vallier can lay out both options side by side so you can see exactly what you are paying and exactly what you are getting in return.
