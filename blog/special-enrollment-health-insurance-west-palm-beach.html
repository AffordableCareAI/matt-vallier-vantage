What Is a Special Enrollment Period? (And Why It Matters Right Now)

A Special Enrollment Period (SEP) is a window outside the regular November-January open enrollment when you can sign up for health insurance through the ACA marketplace (HealthCare.gov, 2026). If you lost your job — or experienced another major life change — you qualify for one.

Why does this matter right now in West Palm Beach? Palm Beach County is feeling the effects of a tightening economy. The unemployment rate hit 5.0% in November 2025, with 38,994 residents out of work — a sharp jump from 3.5% the previous year (BLS, 2025). That's nearly 39,000 people who potentially lost employer-sponsored health coverage.

Here's the part that catches most people off guard: your SEP window is only 60 days. Miss it, and you're stuck waiting until the next open enrollment in November 2026. That could mean months without coverage for you and your family.

From our experience: Most people we talk to in West Palm Beach assume they're stuck without coverage until November. In reality, enrolling through a Special Enrollment Period typically takes a single 30-minute appointment with a licensed agent.

Do You Qualify? The Complete List of Qualifying Life Events

Job loss is the most common trigger, but it isn't the only one. The ACA recognizes several qualifying life events (QLEs) that open a Special Enrollment Period (HealthInsurance.org, 2026).

Loss of Coverage

  • You lost employer health insurance (laid off, fired, hours reduced, or company closed)
  • Your COBRA coverage ran out
  • You aged off a parent's plan at 26
  • You lost Medicaid or CHIP eligibility
  • Your insurance company left the marketplace in your area

Family Changes

  • You got married or divorced
  • You had a baby, adopted a child, or placed a child in foster care
  • A covered family member passed away

Moving

  • You permanently moved to a new zip code or county (relocating to West Palm Beach from out of state absolutely qualifies)
  • You moved back from living abroad

Other Qualifying Events

  • Your household income changed enough to affect subsidy eligibility
  • You gained citizenship or lawful immigration status
  • You were released from incarceration
  • You were affected by a federally declared disaster

The 60-day rule applies to most of these events. For births and adoptions, coverage can start the day of the event. For Medicaid loss, some states allow 90 days. You'll need documentation — a termination letter, a marriage certificate, or proof of your new West Palm Beach address.

COBRA vs. ACA Marketplace: Which Is Actually Cheaper?

For most families, the ACA marketplace wins — and it's not even close. COBRA lets you keep your exact employer plan, but you pay the full premium yourself (employer share included) plus a 2% administrative fee.

The average family saves $800 to $1,200 per month by choosing an ACA marketplace plan over COBRA (OpenExamPrep, 2026). For a healthy 35-year-old, a marketplace plan often costs 40-60% less per month than COBRA with comparable coverage.

When COBRA Still Makes Sense

  • You're in the middle of cancer treatment, surgery recovery, or another ongoing medical situation
  • You've already paid $3,000 to $5,000 toward your annual deductible
  • Your specific doctor or hospital isn't in any ACA marketplace plan network
  • You only need 1-2 months of bridge coverage before new employer insurance starts

But for the majority of West Palm Beach families losing employer coverage, the marketplace is the smarter play — and you may qualify for subsidies that reduce your monthly premium significantly.

How to Enroll in ACA Health Insurance in West Palm Beach (Step by Step)

Step 1: Gather Your Documents

  • Proof of your qualifying event (termination letter, COBRA election notice, or move documentation)
  • Social Security numbers for everyone who needs coverage
  • Income information (last pay stub, unemployment benefits letter, or estimated 2026 income)
  • Immigration documents if applicable

Step 2: Choose Your Path

Option A: Do it yourself at HealthCare.gov. Create an account, report your qualifying life event, and the system will verify eligibility and show you available plans in Palm Beach County.

Option B: Work with a licensed local agent (free). Plans are the same price whether you use an agent or go direct. The difference is local expertise — knowing which Palm Beach County plans cover your doctors, your prescriptions, and which plan actually fits your budget.

Step 3: Compare Plans and Enroll

When comparing plans, pay attention to:

  • Monthly premium — what you pay every month
  • Deductible — what you pay before insurance kicks in
  • Network — are your current doctors and hospitals covered?
  • Prescription coverage — are your medications on the formulary?
  • Out-of-pocket maximum — the most you'd pay in a worst-case scenario

Step 4: Set Your Coverage Start Date

If you enroll before the 15th of any month, coverage starts the first of the following month. Enroll after the 15th, and it starts the first of the month after that. Don't wait until day 59. Processing takes time, and if paperwork is incomplete, you could miss your window entirely.

What About ACA Subsidies in 2026? The Subsidy Cliff, Explained

This is the biggest change in 2026, and it's hitting Florida harder than almost any other state. The enhanced premium tax credits that were in place since 2021 expired on December 31, 2025 (KFF, 2026).

$1,904 average subsidized ACA enrollee annual premium in 2026 (up from $888 in 2025) Source: Kaiser Family Foundation (KFF)

ACA enrollment in Florida dropped from 4.74 million in 2025 to roughly 4.5 million for 2026 — about 197,000 fewer Floridians covered (KFF, 2026).

Do You Still Qualify for Subsidies?

Yes — subsidies still exist. They're just smaller than they were in 2024 and 2025:

  • Income between 100% and 400% of the Federal Poverty Level — roughly $15,060 to $60,240 for a single person; about $31,200 to $124,800 for a family of four
  • Not eligible for employer insurance, Medicare, or Medicaid
  • Filing taxes as required

If you just lost your job, your projected annual income for 2026 may be significantly lower than your previous salary. That works in your favor — lower income means larger subsidies.

The Four Plan Tiers

  • Bronze — Lowest premium, highest out-of-pocket. Best if you're young, healthy, need catastrophic coverage.
  • Silver — Mid-range. Best value for most because cost-sharing reductions (CSRs) only apply to Silver plans.
  • Gold — Higher premium, lower deductible. Good if you use healthcare regularly.
  • Platinum — Highest premium, lowest out-of-pocket. Best for frequent users or chronic conditions.

For most people who just lost their job and are watching their budget, a Silver plan with a subsidy is the sweet spot.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I get health insurance after losing my job in Florida?

You qualify for a 60-day Special Enrollment Period through the ACA marketplace at HealthCare.gov. You can also work with a licensed local agent at no extra cost. You'll need your termination letter and income information to apply.

What is a Special Enrollment Period?

A SEP is a window outside the regular November-January open enrollment when you can sign up for ACA health insurance. It's triggered by qualifying life events like job loss, marriage, having a baby, or moving. Most SEPs last 60 days from the event.

Is COBRA or the ACA marketplace cheaper?

For most people, the ACA marketplace is significantly cheaper. COBRA runs $700-$900/month for an individual; marketplace plans with subsidies often cost $200-$500/month. The average family saves $800-$1,200 monthly on the marketplace.

Do I qualify for ACA subsidies in 2026?

If your household income falls between 100% and 400% of the Federal Poverty Level (about $15,060 to $60,240 for a single person), you likely qualify. Enhanced subsidies expired December 2025, so subsidies are smaller than previous years — but still significant.

How long do I have to enroll after losing my job?

Exactly 60 days from the date you lose employer coverage. Don't wait until the last minute — processing delays can cause you to miss the window. Start the application within the first two weeks.

Can I get health insurance in West Palm Beach without waiting for open enrollment?

Yes. Job loss, moving to a new area, getting married, having a baby, and several other life events trigger a Special Enrollment Period that lets you enroll immediately.

What happens if I miss the 60-day Special Enrollment window?

You'll have to wait until the next open enrollment (November 2026) for marketplace coverage. Other options: Medicaid if you qualify, short-term health plans with limited coverage, or a new qualifying life event.
